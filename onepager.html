Fleet-Based Services & Service Station Modernization

Mesa’s current code was written for traditional gas stations. These proposed amendments would add clear definitions, say where each use belongs, and set modern development standards for drone delivery, robo-taxi & delivery fleets, and private-sector EV charging, with safeguards for nearby neighborhoods. Figures shown are proposed starting points and may change with stakeholder feedback.

① Fleet-Based Services NEW / REVISED

Sites that park, stage, dispatch, fuel & charge a fleet, ground or aerial (incl. drones).

  • Light: ≤ 24 vehicles < 10,000 lbs
  • Heavy (new): > 24 vehicles, or any > 10,000 lbs
Where allowed
Light: LCGCLIGIHI DB-2DC (CUP)
Heavy: GCGIHI

② Accessory EV Charging NEW

Lets a normal business add EV charging that stays incidental to its main use.

  • Capped at 16 charging spaces
  • May not take required parking
  • Keeps it from becoming a service station
Where allowed
NCLCGCOCMX PEPLIGIHI DB-1DB-2DC

③ Service Stations MODERNIZED

Now serves the public with gas, diesel or electricity (EVSE), not private fleets.

  • Canopy ≤ 16 ft; fascia ≤ 30 in
  • Recessed lighting; vehicle stacking rules
  • Max 2 per arterial intersection
Key idea
Separates public fueling/charging from private fleet operations.

Neighborhood safeguards

100 ft separation from homes
60 dB max at property line
Screening walls (masonry / metal)
≤ 30 ft aerial fleet yard height
≤ 20 fc lighting near homes
Admin. flexibility via studies
Why not say “drones”?
Arizona (incl. pending HB 2875) reserves drone flight rules to the state & FAA, but cities keep authority over land use. Mesa regulates “aerial-based vehicles” as a land use, within city authority and consistent with pending state law.
